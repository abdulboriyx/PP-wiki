# Concept: Sovereignty

**Type:** Political Concept

---

## Definition

Sovereignty refers to the supreme, absolute, and uncontrollable power by which an independent state is governed. It is the ultimate source of legal and political authority within a territory.

---

## Hobbesian Sovereignty (The Leviathan)

In *[Leviathan](../sources/hobbes-leviathan.md)*, [Thomas Hobbes](../thinkers/hobbes.md) provides the definitive defense of absolute sovereignty as the only solution to the [State of Nature](state-of-nature.md).

### Characteristics
1. **Absolute**: The sovereign is not bound by any laws (since they are the author of the laws) or by the subjects (since the contract is among the subjects to obey the sovereign, not a contract between the subjects and the sovereign).
2. **Indivisible**: Power cannot be shared between different branches of government. Hobbes argues that divided sovereignty leads to disagreement and civil war.
3. **Inalienable**: The sovereign cannot transfer their rights or duties to another without ceasing to be sovereign.
4. **Ultimate Judge**: The sovereign has the right to judge what opinions and doctrines are adverse to peace, and to regulate speech and religion accordingly.

### The Purpose
The primary purpose of the sovereign is the **safety of the people** (*salus populi*). If the sovereign fails to provide security, or becomes the direct source of a subject's destruction, the subject's obligation to obey ceases, as the right to self-preservation is inalienable.

---

## Other Interpretations

- **Jean Bodin**: Often credited with the first modern formulation of sovereignty, defining it as the "absolute and perpetual power of a Commonwealth."
- **Popular Sovereignty (Rousseau)**: Argues that sovereignty belongs to the people as a whole (the general will), rather than a single individual or elite.
- **Limited Sovereignty (Locke)**: Sovereignty is delegated to the government by the people, and is limited by the natural rights of individuals. If the government exceeds its mandate, the people have a right to revolt.
