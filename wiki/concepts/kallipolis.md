# Concept: Kallipolis

## Definition

Kallipolis is the ideal city constructed by [Plato](../thinkers/plato.md) in *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*. It is not introduced as a practical constitution first, but as a model for seeing [Justice](justice.md) on a larger scale.

## Structure

- **Rulers:** Philosophers who know the [Form of the Good](form-of-good.md).
- **Auxiliaries:** Guardian-soldiers who defend the city and enforce rational rule.
- **Producers:** Farmers, craftsmen, merchants, and others who provide material needs.

## Interpretations

- **Epistemic interpretation:** Kallipolis shows that political authority should be guided by knowledge rather than appetite, wealth, or opinion.
- **Psychological interpretation:** The city mirrors the [Tripartite Soul](tripartite-soul.md): rulers correspond to reason, auxiliaries to spirit, and producers to appetite.
- **Authoritarian interpretation:** Kallipolis is a hierarchical regime that subordinates individual liberty, family life, poetry, and economic desire to civic unity.

## Tensions

Kallipolis is both a model of psychic order and a political hierarchy. This creates a disagreement between Platonic justice as function and modern theories that treat political legitimacy as consent, equality, or rights.
