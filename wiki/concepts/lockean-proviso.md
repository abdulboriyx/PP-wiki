# Concept: Lockean Proviso

**Type:** Constraint on initial bargaining position within contractarianism

---

## Definition

The Lockean Proviso is a condition originating in Locke's political philosophy, adapted by Gauthier and others as a constraint on the initial bargaining situation in contractarian theory. In its standard form:

> One may appropriate or benefit oneself only provided one does not thereby worsen the situation of others.

In Gauthier's formulation: the initial bargaining position for a fair contract must have been arrived at non-coercively — no party may have improved their position by worsening another's. Agreements reached from a proviso-satisfying baseline are more likely to secure stable compliance.

---

## Function in Contractarian Theory

The proviso does two things:

1. **Rules out dominance and exploitation as inputs**: Historical patterns of theft, coercion, or exploitation cannot form the starting point of a fair contract. If one party has bettered themselves by worsening others, the resulting agreement reflects that injustice.

2. **Grounds fairness without a veil of ignorance**: Unlike Rawls's original position, Gauthier's approach does not require that contractors be ignorant of their attributes. Instead, fairness is secured by requiring that holdings at the baseline position are non-coercively acquired.

---

## Limitations

Even with the proviso, inequality in natural talents and abilities remains. Gauthier acknowledges that eliminating coercively acquired advantages still leaves natural inequalities that can generate bargaining-power asymmetries — and thus outcomes that favor the naturally advantaged.

**Impartiality objection**: Even if all dominance relations are eliminated, natural talent asymmetries re-introduce the possibility of dominance, undermining the claim that outcomes are truly fair.

---

## Relation to Subversive Contractarianism

Pateman and Mills argue that the real historical baseline is thoroughly contaminated by coercion — the sexual and racial contracts were coercive agreements that formed the actual starting point of modern liberal politics. The Lockean Proviso would, if applied rigorously, condemn most existing property distributions and political arrangements.
