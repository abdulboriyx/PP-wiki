# Concept: Form of the Good

## Definition

The Form of the Good is Plato's highest object of philosophical knowledge in *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*. It is presented as the source that makes truth knowable and things intelligible, using the analogy of the sun.

## Platonic Role

The Good is not simply one good thing among others. It is the standard by which rulers understand what makes a city, law, education, and soul properly ordered. This is why knowledge of the Good is required for [Philosopher Kings](philosopher-king.md).

## Interpretations

- **Metaphysical interpretation:** The Good is the highest principle of reality and intelligibility.
- **Political interpretation:** The Good is the standard that prevents rule from collapsing into appetite, honor, wealth, or majority opinion.
- **Epistemic interpretation:** Without knowledge of the Good, rulers handle appearances rather than causes.

## Tensions

The Good grounds Plato's defense of philosopher-rule, but the dialogue does not give a simple definition of it. This leaves a problem: political authority depends on knowledge that is difficult to publicly verify.
