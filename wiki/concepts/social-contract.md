# Concept: Social Contract

**Type:** Political and moral concept (umbrella)

---

## Definition

The social contract is a theoretical device used to ground the legitimacy of political authority and/or moral norms by reference to the idea of agreement or consent among individuals. It encompasses two distinct theoretical strains:

- [Contractarianism](contractarianism.md) — Hobbesian; grounded in rational self-interest
- [Contractualism](contractualism.md) — Kantian; grounded in public justifiability

Both share the core idea that political and moral obligations must derive from agreement rather than divine will or perfectionist ideals.

---

## The Metaphor of Contract

The contract metaphor requires specifying answers to three questions:

1. **What is the agreement on?** Options include:
   - Principles of justice (Rousseau, Rawls)
   - Basic social institutions (Rawls)
   - Alienation of rights to a sovereign (Hobbes, Locke)
   - Adoption of a disposition to be moral (Gauthier, Hampton)

2. **How is the agreement to be understood?**
   - Actual historical event
   - Possible historical moment
   - Purely hypothetical thought experiment

3. **Is the contract justificatory or explanatory?**
   - Normative contractarianism: the contract *justifies* moral and political norms
   - Subversive contractarianism (Pateman, Mills): the contract *explains* the origin of oppression (patriarchy, white supremacy) — see [Debate: Subversive Contractarianism](../debates/subversive-contractarianism.md)

---

## Historical vs. Hypothetical Contract

A persistent controversy concerns whether a hypothetical contract can do normative work:

- **Hume's objection**: Even if our ancestors consented, that consent cannot bind us.
- **Dworkin's objection**: A hypothetical contract is not a contract at all; it lacks binding force.
- **Gauthier's response**: The contract is a heuristic for discovering the requirements of practical rationality — if one is rational among rational others where cooperation is beneficial, rationality requires abiding by the contract's terms.

---

## Key Elements of Any Social Contract Theory

1. **Initial situation**: The [state of nature](state-of-nature.md) or baseline; the no-agreement point.
2. **Characterization of parties**: Their rationality, motivation, and informational situation.
3. **Agreement principle**: The rule by which the contract's content is determined (minimax concession, maximin, etc.).
4. **Scope**: Who counts as a legitimate party to the contract.

## Historical Thinkers

- [Thomas Hobbes](../thinkers/hobbes.md) — state of nature as war; alienation contract; sovereignty
- [John Locke](../thinkers/locke.md) — natural rights; limited government; Lockean proviso
- [Jean-Jacques Rousseau](../thinkers/rousseau.md) — general will; principles of justice
- [Immanuel Kant](../thinkers/kant.md) — moral law; kingdom of ends; foundation for contractualism

## Contemporary Thinkers

- [John Rawls](../thinkers/rawls.md) — original position; veil of ignorance; contractualism
- [David Gauthier](../thinkers/gauthier.md) — Morals by Agreement; contractarianism
- [Carole Pateman](../thinkers/pateman.md) — Sexual Contract; subversive contractarianism
- [Charles W. Mills](../thinkers/mills-charles.md) — Racial Contract; subversive contractarianism
