# Concept: Noble Lie

## Definition

A "noble lie" (gennaion pseudos) is a myth or untruth, often, but not invariably, of a religious nature, knowingly propagated by an elite to maintain social harmony or advance an agenda. 

## Platonic Context

The concept originates in [Plato's](../thinkers/plato.md) *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*. Socrates proposes that the rulers of the ideal city must invent a foundational myth—the Myth of the Metals. 

The myth claims that all citizens were born from the earth, making them siblings, but the gods mixed different metals into their souls:
- **Gold:** For the rulers ([Philosopher Kings](../concepts/philosopher-king.md))
- **Silver:** For the auxiliaries (soldiers)
- **Iron/Bronze:** For the producers (farmers, craftsmen)

## Purpose

The Noble Lie serves two functions:
1. To foster patriotism and a sense of shared brotherhood among the citizens.
2. To justify and cement the rigid social hierarchy, making citizens content with their assigned class and duties.

## Claim / Interpretation / Evidence

- **Claim:** Civic unity may require a shared founding myth.
- **Interpretation:** Plato treats political order as dependent not only on rational knowledge among rulers but also on socially binding belief among citizens.
- **Evidence:** The myth of metals links naturalized hierarchy to the class structure of the [Kallipolis](kallipolis.md).

## Tension

The noble lie conflicts with Plato's commitment to philosophical truth. It raises the question whether a just city can be founded on deliberate falsehood.
