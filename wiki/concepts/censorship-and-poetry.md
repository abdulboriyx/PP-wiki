# Concept: Censorship and Poetry

## Definition

In *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*, censorship is the regulation or exclusion of poetic material from the ideal city because poetry shapes character and can strengthen irrational parts of the soul.

## Platonic Interpretation

Plato's argument has two stages:

1. **Book III:** Stories for future guardians must not portray gods, heroes, death, grief, or vice in ways that corrupt courage and moderation.
2. **Book X:** Imitative poetry is excluded because it is far from truth and appeals to emotion rather than reason.

## Different Interpretations

- **Educational interpretation:** Censorship protects the formation of guardian character.
- **Psychological interpretation:** Poetry is dangerous because imitation trains the soul to indulge grief, anger, appetite, and illusion.
- **Authoritarian interpretation:** Plato subordinates artistic freedom to political control.

## Tensions

Plato attacks myth and imitation, yet *The Republic* itself uses myths, images, and dramatic imitation. This tension is central to [Poetry and Political Education](../debates/poetry-and-political-education.md).
