# Concept: Philosopher King

## Definition

A philosopher king is a ruler who possesses both political power and philosophical knowledge. The concept was introduced by [Plato](../thinkers/plato.md) in his dialogue *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*.

## Core Idea

Plato argues that the ideal state (Kallipolis) can only exist when "philosophers become kings in the world, or until those we now call kings and rulers really and truly become philosophers." 

The philosopher king is uniquely qualified to rule because they have undergone rigorous education (including mathematics and dialectic) to grasp the Form of the Good. Unlike ordinary politicians who are swayed by public opinion and appearances, the philosopher king possesses true knowledge and therefore governs purely for the benefit of the whole state, rather than out of self-interest.
