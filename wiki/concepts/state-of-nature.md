# Concept: State of Nature

**Type:** Theoretical construct within social contract theory

---

## Definition

The state of nature is the hypothetical or conjectural condition of human beings prior to (or in the absence of) political authority, law, and enforceable social norms. In social contract theory, it functions as the *initial situation* — the no-agreement baseline that gives agents reason to contract.

Also called: *initial bargaining position* (Gauthier), *original position* (Rawls, though Rawls adds a veil of ignorance and other features).

---

## Functions in Contract Theory

1. **Motivational**: Shows why rational agents would want to leave the pre-political condition and agree to norms.
2. **Normative baseline**: Defines what each party is entitled to if negotiations fail (the "no-agreement point" in bargaining theory).
3. **Scope-setting**: Who is present in the state of nature determines who counts as a party to the contract.

---

## Interpretations

### Hobbes — War of All Against All
The state of nature is a condition of perpetual conflict driven by competition, diffidence, and glory-seeking. Life is "solitary, poore, nasty, brutish, and short." Any agreement, even to an authoritarian sovereign, is preferable. The severe baseline justifies strong concessions to government.

### Locke — Imperfect Natural Rights
The state of nature is governed by natural law (God's law). People have rights to life, liberty, and property; these pre-exist government. Government is justified only insofar as it protects these rights. The baseline is less hostile than Hobbes's.

### Rousseau — Natural Goodness
Human beings in the state of nature are naturally good; corruption is a product of society and inequality. The social contract must recover, not merely escape, natural freedom.

### Gauthier — Initial Bargaining Position
Not a war, but a situation of independent individual action with no moral constraints. The Lockean proviso limits the initial position: no one may have bettered themselves by worsening others. This ensures the initial bargaining position is non-coercive.

### Rawls — Original Position
Rawls transforms the state-of-nature device: the original position is not a pre-social condition but a hypothetical standpoint behind a *veil of ignorance*, from which parties choose principles of justice without knowing their social position or natural talents. This ensures impartiality.

---

## Key Controversies

- **Descriptive vs. normative**: Is the state of nature meant as a historical claim about how humans once lived, or a normative device for generating principles? (Hobbes and Locke suggested both; Gauthier and Rawls are explicitly normative/hypothetical.)
- **Severity of the baseline**: The worse the state of nature, the more concessions to authority are rational — raising the risk that contractarianism licenses authoritarianism.
- **Who is included**: Only those present in the initial situation become parties to the contract. This generates the [inclusion/exclusion problem](../debates/inclusion-exclusion.md).
