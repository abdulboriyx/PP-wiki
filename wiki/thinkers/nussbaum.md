# Thinker: Martha Nussbaum

**Tradition:** Capabilities approach; liberal political philosophy; feminist philosophy  
**Key Works:** *Women and Human Development*, *Frontiers of Justice*, *Creating Capabilities*

---

## Core Ideas

Martha Nussbaum is important for this wiki as a critic of contract theories that make reciprocity or productive contribution central to justice. Her capabilities approach argues that justice should secure central human capabilities, including for people whose claims cannot be grounded in mutual advantage bargaining.

In the context of [Contractarianism](../concepts/contractarianism.md), Nussbaum is especially relevant to the [Inclusion and Exclusion](../debates/inclusion-exclusion.md) debate. She challenges theories that struggle to include people with severe disabilities, animals, and global justice claims.
