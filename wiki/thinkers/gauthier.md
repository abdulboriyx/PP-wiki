# Thinker: David Gauthier (b. 1932)

**Tradition:** Analytic philosophy; contractarianism; moral theory

---

## Core Ideas

Gauthier is the most important contemporary *contractarian* (as opposed to contractualist) moral philosopher. His project is to ground morality in rationality — to defeat the moral skeptic who asks "why be moral?" — using the tools of bargaining theory and game theory.

### 1. Morals by Agreement (1986)

**Problem**: Cooperative activities face the prisoner's dilemma — each party does best by defecting while the other cooperates, leading to a suboptimal outcome for all.

**Solution**: Rational agents will *dispose themselves* to be **constrained maximizers** — they adopt a disposition to keep agreements whenever they find themselves among like-minded cooperators, rather than always directly maximizing self-interest. This disposition is itself rational because it enables mutually beneficial cooperation.

**Agreement principle — Minimax Relative Concession**: Each bargainer is most concerned with relative concessions. The rational agreement is the one that minimizes the maximum relative concession any party must make. A relative concession = how much you concede from your ideal outcome, measured as a proportion of your possible range of outcomes.

**Initial position — Modified Lockean Proviso**: The baseline must be arrived at non-coercively. No party may have bettered themselves by worsening others. (See [Lockean Proviso](../concepts/lockean-proviso.md).)

**Liberal individual**: In the final chapter of *Morals by Agreement*, Gauthier sketches the "liberal individual" whose moral psychology is shaped by living under mutual advantage contractarianism — someone who can cooperate genuinely, not merely strategically.

### 2. Revised View (2013)

Gauthier subsequently rejected the bargaining-problem framing. Under his revised view:
- Parties are **Pareto-optimizers** (rational cooperators), not constrained maximizers.
- A Pareto-optimal outcome: no party's payoff can be increased without decreasing another's.
- Agreement principle becomes **Maximin Proportionate Gain**: select the outcome that maximizes the minimum proportionate gain (actual cooperative gain / potential cooperative gain) of any single party.

---

## Critiques of Gauthier

- **Hampton**: Constrained maximization requires that agents retrain their motivations — but it's unclear rational agents would or could do this.
- **Moehler (2020)**: The move to constrained maximization *moralizes* practical reasoning, departing from strict contractarianism.
- **Compliance problem**: Vallentyne (1991) and others find the dispositional solution to the compliance problem dubitable.
- **Impartiality**: Even with the Lockean Proviso, natural talent inequalities re-introduce bargaining-power asymmetries.
- **Exclusion**: Those who cannot contribute to mutual advantage (severely disabled, animals) fall outside Gauthier's scope of justice.
