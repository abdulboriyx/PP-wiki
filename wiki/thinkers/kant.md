# Thinker: Immanuel Kant (1724–1804)

**Tradition:** German Idealism; deontological ethics; contractualism

---

## Core Ideas

1. **Rationality requires respect for persons**: Rational agents must treat persons as ends in themselves, never merely as means. This grounds morality in reason rather than self-interest.

2. **Categorical Imperative**: Act only on maxims you could will to be universal laws. The contractualist interpretation: moral principles must be justifiable to all rational agents.

3. **Kingdom of Ends**: A community of rational beings who legislate moral law for themselves. Each member is both subject to and author of the moral law. This is the precursor to contractualism's emphasis on mutual justifiability.

4. **Foundation for contractualism**: Kant's emphasis on public justifiability of moral principles to rational agents forms the basis of the contractualist strain of social contract theory, developed later by Rawls and Scanlon.

---

## Kant vs. Hobbes (Contractualism vs. Contractarianism)

Where Hobbes grounds moral norms in rational self-interest, Kant grounds them in the requirement that each person be treated as a rational agent worthy of justification. Compliance is motivated not by fear of others' depredations but by respect for persons as such.
