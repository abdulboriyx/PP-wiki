# Thinker: Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778)

**Tradition:** Enlightenment political philosophy; social contract theory

---

## Core Ideas

1. **Natural goodness and social corruption**: Humans are naturally good; moral and political corruption arises from inequality and the corrupting effects of civil society. Unlike Hobbes, Rousseau does not see the state of nature as a war — it is peaceful but pre-moral.

2. **General will**: Legitimate political authority derives from the *general will* — the will of the community as a whole aimed at the common good, distinct from the mere sum of individual preferences (*will of all*). Political obligation binds individuals because they are, in a sense, authors of the laws they obey.

3. **Social contract as principles of justice**: For Rousseau, the agreement is on the principles of justice that will constitute the legitimate political order, not merely on the terms of peaceful coexistence (as in Hobbes).

4. **Freedom as self-governance**: True freedom is not mere absence of external constraint but participation in self-governing political community. Civil freedom replaces natural freedom.
