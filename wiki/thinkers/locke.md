# Thinker: John Locke (1632–1704)

**Tradition:** Early modern political philosophy; social contract theory; natural rights liberalism

---

## Core Ideas

1. **Natural rights and limited government**: Unlike Hobbes, Locke holds that the state of nature is governed by natural law — persons have natural rights to life, liberty, and property prior to political authority. Government is legitimate only insofar as it protects these pre-political rights.

2. **Lockean Proviso**: Appropriation of resources is legitimate only provided one does not worsen the situation of others (originally, provided "enough and as good" is left for others). This constraint has been adapted by contractarians, especially Gauthier, as a limit on the initial bargaining position.

3. **Consent and political authority**: Political authority requires the consent of the governed. A government that violates natural rights forfeits its legitimacy and may be overthrown.

4. **Delegation, not alienation**: Unlike Hobbes, Locke's contract is a *delegation*, not an *alienation*: citizens lend, not permanently transfer, their authority. The government is a trustee; the people retain ultimate sovereignty.
