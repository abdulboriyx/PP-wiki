# Thinker: Aristotle

**Tradition:** Classical Greek Philosophy  
**Key Works:** *Politics*, *Nicomachean Ethics*, *Poetics*, *Rhetoric*, *Metaphysics*

---

## Core Ideas

Aristotle was Plato's student and later one of his most important critics. In political philosophy, Aristotle treats the polis as a natural community ordered toward human flourishing, but he is less utopian than [Plato](plato.md) and gives more attention to existing constitutions, mixed regimes, citizenship, and practical judgment.

For this wiki's current Plato material, Aristotle is most relevant as a contrast on poetry and imitation. Where Plato worries that imitative poetry corrupts the soul and should be excluded from the [Kallipolis](../concepts/kallipolis.md), Aristotle's *Poetics* treats tragedy as a structured art that can clarify action and educate emotion.
