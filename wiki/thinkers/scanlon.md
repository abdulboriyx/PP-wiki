# Thinker: T. M. Scanlon

**Tradition:** Contractualism; moral philosophy  
**Key Works:** *What We Owe to Each Other*

---

## Core Ideas

T. M. Scanlon is a major contemporary contractualist. His central idea is that moral principles are those that no one could reasonably reject as a basis for informed, unforced general agreement.

For this wiki, Scanlon helps distinguish [Contractualism](../concepts/contractualism.md) from [Contractarianism](../concepts/contractarianism.md). Unlike Hobbesian contractarianism, Scanlon's contractualism does not ground morality in mutual advantage among self-interested bargainers.
