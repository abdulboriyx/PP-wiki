# Thinker: Jan Narveson (b. 1936)

**Tradition:** Analytic philosophy; libertarian contractarianism; moral theory

---

## Core Ideas

Narveson represents the libertarian wing of contractarianism. His work develops the Hobbesian insight that the primary motivation for social contract is fear of the depredations of others — rather than the potential gains from positive cooperation.

### Libertarian Contractarianism
When the primary contractarian motivation is *vulnerability to others* (rather than gains from cooperation), the minimal rational agreement is one that secures basic security against aggression. This yields:
- Near-absolute property rights
- Minimal government (limited to protection of persons and property)
- No redistributive obligations — taxation beyond minimal security provision is not supported by the contractarian logic

Contrast with Gauthier, where the emphasis on *gains from cooperation* opens the door to more extensive social institutions.

---

## The Narveson Fork
The source identifies a "Narveson fork" implicit in Hobbesian contractarianism:
- **Fear-primary contractarianism** → Narveson's libertarianism
- **Benefit-primary contractarianism** → Gauthier's liberal individual; more expansive reciprocity and supportive government
