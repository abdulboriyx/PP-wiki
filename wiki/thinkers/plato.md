# Thinker: Plato

**Tradition:** Classical Greek Philosophy
**Key Works:** *The Republic*, *The Laws*, *Statesman*, *Symposium*, *Phaedo*

---

## Core Ideas

Plato (c. 428–348 BC) was an Athenian philosopher during the Classical period in Ancient Greece, founder of the Platonist school of thought and the Academy.

His political philosophy is primarily articulated in *[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)*, where he explores the nature of justice and the characteristics of a just city-state, the [Kallipolis](../concepts/kallipolis.md), and a just individual. He famously proposed that the ideal state should be ruled by [Philosopher Kings](../concepts/philosopher-king.md), individuals who have attained knowledge of the [Form of the Good](../concepts/form-of-good.md).

Plato's account of [Justice](../concepts/justice.md) links politics to psychology. The just city has rulers, auxiliaries, and producers performing their proper functions; the just soul has reason, spirit, and appetite ordered in the same way. This makes his theory of the state inseparable from the [Tripartite Soul](../concepts/tripartite-soul.md).

He argued against democracy, comparing democratic politics to a ship taken over by a crew ignorant of navigation. Instead, he favored a rigid class structure maintained partly by education and partly by the [Noble Lie](../concepts/noble-lie.md). His critique of democracy is not only institutional but psychological: a regime that treats all desires as equal tends toward disorder and eventually tyranny.

Plato also gives political education a central role. The [Allegory of the Cave](../concepts/allegory-of-the-cave.md) presents education as the turning of the soul toward truth, while his account of [Censorship and Poetry](../concepts/censorship-and-poetry.md) treats art as a force that can either support or corrupt civic character.

## Tensions

- Plato grounds rule in knowledge, but this creates the problem of how non-philosophers could verify that rulers truly know the Good.
- Plato defends truth-seeking philosophy while allowing the [Noble Lie](../concepts/noble-lie.md) as a civic myth.
- Plato criticizes poetry and imitation while using myths and images as philosophical teaching devices.
