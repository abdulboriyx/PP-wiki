# Thinker: Plato

**Tradition:** Classical Greek Philosophy
**Key Works:** *The Republic*, *The Laws*, *Statesman*, *Symposium*, *Phaedo*

---

## Core Ideas

Plato (c. 428–348 BC) was an Athenian philosopher during the Classical period in Ancient Greece, founder of the Platonist school of thought and the Academy.

His political philosophy is primarily articulated in *The Republic*, where he explores the nature of justice and the characteristics of a just city-state (Kallipolis) and a just individual. He famously proposed that the ideal state should be ruled by [Philosopher Kings](../concepts/philosopher-king.md), individuals who have attained knowledge of the Good.

He argued against democracy, comparing it to a ship taken over by a mutinous crew ignorant of navigation. Instead, he favored a rigid class structure maintained by a [Noble Lie](../concepts/noble-lie.md). His theory of the state is deeply intertwined with his psychology, proposing a [Tripartite Soul](../concepts/tripartite-soul.md) analogous to the three classes in the state.
