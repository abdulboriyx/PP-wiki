# Argument: Justice as Harmony in The Republic

**Source:** [The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)  
**Thinker:** [Plato](../thinkers/plato.md)  
**Concepts:** [Justice](../concepts/justice.md), [Tripartite Soul](../concepts/tripartite-soul.md), [Kallipolis](../concepts/kallipolis.md)

---

## Claim

Justice is the condition in which each part of a city or soul performs its proper function under rational order.

---

## Evidence

Book IV identifies justice after locating wisdom, courage, and moderation in the city. The remaining virtue is the principle that each class does its own work. Socrates then applies the same pattern to the individual soul: reason rules, spirit supports reason, and appetite obeys.

---

## Explanation

The city is an enlarged model that lets Socrates identify justice more clearly. Once justice is visible in the city, it can be read back into the soul. This makes justice an internal order rather than merely a set of external actions.

---

## What This Does Not Prove

It does not prove that Plato's class hierarchy is morally acceptable. It also does not answer modern egalitarian objections that political order should begin from equal standing rather than natural function.
