# Argument: Justice as Harmony in The Republic

## Argument in One Sentence

Justice is the order in which each part of the city and soul does its own work under rational rule.

## Source

[The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)  
**Author:** [Plato](../thinkers/plato.md)  
**Pages / Section:** Book IV, PDF pp. 275-304

## Original Text Fragment

> "justice was doing one's own business, and not being a busybody"

## Why This Is an Argument

Socrates moves from political function to psychic order. He first identifies a city as just when rulers, auxiliaries, and producers each perform their proper roles. He then argues that the individual soul is also divided and that justice in the person must have the same structure.

## How the Text Supports the Argument

The fragment names the principle that connects division of labor, class structure, and moral order. It supports the argument because it states justice as a relation among parts rather than a list of outward actions.

## Broader Explanation

This is the central constructive answer to the challenge of Book II. Justice is not merely useful because others reward it; it is good because it makes the soul internally ordered.

The [Kallipolis](../concepts/kallipolis.md) functions as an enlarged image of the individual. Its classes correspond to the [Tripartite Soul](../concepts/tripartite-soul.md): reason rules, spirit supports, and appetite obeys. Political philosophy and moral psychology become mutually dependent.

## Underlying Assumptions

- Cities and souls have distinguishable parts with proper functions.
- Order is better than internal conflict.
- Reason is naturally entitled to rule appetite and spirit.

## What This Argument Does Not Prove

It does not prove that Plato's social hierarchy is just by modern standards. It also does not prove that real people fit neatly into fixed civic classes. The argument is conceptual and normative, not an empirical sociology of actual cities.

## Opposing Views

- **Thrasymachus:** Justice is the advantage of the stronger, not objective harmony.
- **Democratic egalitarianism:** Political justice should begin from equal standing, not unequal function.
- **Contractualism:** Justice is based on principles no one could reasonably reject, not psychic hierarchy.
