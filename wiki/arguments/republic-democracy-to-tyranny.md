# Argument: Democracy Tends Toward Tyranny in The Republic

**Source:** [The Republic](../sources/plato-republic.md)  
**Thinker:** [Plato](../thinkers/plato.md)  
**Concepts:** [Justice](../concepts/justice.md), [Tripartite Soul](../concepts/tripartite-soul.md)

---

## Claim

Democracy can decay into tyranny when freedom becomes the absence of rational order.

---

## Evidence

Books VIII-IX describe democracy as a regime of variety, permissiveness, and equal license among desires. Plato then argues that this condition produces faction and a longing for a protector, who becomes a tyrant.

---

## Explanation

The political argument mirrors Plato's psychology. A democratic soul treats all desires as equal; a tyrannical soul is dominated by lawless appetite. The tyrant is therefore not an accidental ruler but the political form of disordered desire.

---

## What This Does Not Prove

It does not prove that every democracy must become tyranny. Plato's account is a normative and psychological critique, not a neutral empirical law of regime change.
