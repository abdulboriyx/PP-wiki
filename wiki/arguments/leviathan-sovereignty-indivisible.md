# Argument: Sovereignty Must Be Indivisible

## Argument in One Sentence

Hobbes argues that sovereign power must be unified and indivisible because divided authority recreates the conflict it was established to prevent.

## Source

[Leviathan](../sources/hobbes-leviathan.md)  
**Author:** [Thomas Hobbes](../thinkers/hobbes.md)  
**Pages / Section:** Part II, Chapters XVIII-XXIX, PDF around pp. 103-205

## Original Text Fragment

> "the sovereign power may be divided"

## Why This Is an Argument

Hobbes treats divided sovereignty as a contradiction in the structure of authority. If two powers claim final judgment, subjects are returned to private judgment about which authority to obey. The sovereign must therefore be the final judge of law, doctrine, war, peace, and public order.

## How the Text Supports the Argument

The fragment is part of Hobbes's list of doctrines that dissolve commonwealths. It supports the argument because divided sovereignty is identified as a direct threat to political unity.

## Broader Explanation

For Hobbes, the commonwealth is made one by representation. A multitude becomes one political person only through a common representative. If sovereignty is divided, the artificial person fragments and the state tends back toward war.

This argument explains Hobbes's rejection of mixed sovereignty and his suspicion of rival ecclesiastical power. It also creates the major liberal worry about Hobbes: peace is purchased at the cost of extreme concentration of power.

## Underlying Assumptions

- Final authority must exist somewhere in every commonwealth.
- Rival final authorities generate conflict.
- Peace is the first political good.

## What This Argument Does Not Prove

It does not prove that checks and balances are impossible. It also does not prove that a unified sovereign will rule well. It is a structural argument about final authority, not a guarantee of justice.

## Opposing Views

- **Liberal constitutionalism:** Divided powers can prevent tyranny and preserve liberty.
- **Republicanism:** Mixed government can stabilize political life.
- **Democratic theory:** Accountability may require institutional division rather than sovereign unity.
