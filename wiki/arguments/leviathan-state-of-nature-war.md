# Argument: The State of Nature Is a Condition of War

## Argument in One Sentence

Hobbes argues that without a common power, human equality, competition, fear, and glory produce a condition of war.

## Source

[Leviathan](../sources/hobbes-leviathan.md)  
**Author:** [Thomas Hobbes](../thinkers/hobbes.md)  
**Pages / Section:** Part I, Chapter XIII, PDF around p. 76

## Original Text Fragment

> "continual fear, and danger of violent death"

## Why This Is an Argument

Hobbes is not only describing violence. He argues that when no public authority can settle disputes, rational anticipation of danger makes preemptive action reasonable. War is therefore a condition of insecurity, not only active fighting.

## How the Text Supports the Argument

The fragment captures the central condition Hobbes wants to explain: life without common power is dominated by fear and vulnerability. This supports the argument because fear is what makes peace-seeking rational.

## Broader Explanation

Hobbes begins with individuals who are roughly equal enough to threaten one another. Because goods are scarce, intentions are uncertain, and reputation matters, each person has reason to distrust others. The result is a warlike condition where industry, culture, and stable property cannot flourish.

This argument grounds the rest of *Leviathan*. The state of nature is the problem that the [Social Contract](../concepts/social-contract.md) and [Sovereignty](../concepts/sovereignty.md) are meant to solve.

## Underlying Assumptions

- People are vulnerable to one another.
- No one can reliably trust others without enforcement.
- Rational self-preservation can justify preemptive defensive action.

## What This Argument Does Not Prove

It does not prove that actual pre-political history was literally a universal war. It is a theoretical baseline for explaining why authority is rationally necessary.

## Opposing Views

- **Rousseau:** The state of nature is not necessarily Hobbesian war; social comparison and property corrupt human beings.
- **Locke:** The state of nature has natural law and rights before government.
- **Plato:** Disorder arises from psychic and civic misrule, not from a contractarian no-agreement position.
